ABOUT

I'm a scholar. I enjoy scholarly pursuits.

I am a PhD student in philosophy at CUNY Graduate Center. I work on philosophy of language, with an emphasis on semantics, pragmatics, and non-human communication. I also teach at Brooklyn College. In my spare time, I code and make music.

Philosophical background

I am mainly influenced by the Gricean framework of intention-based semantics. My work attempts to unify teleosemantic views of intentionality with intention-based theories of human communication. I also work on formal pragmatics, the semantics-pragmatics distinction, and the topic of slurs.

Academic background

I graduated from Swarthmore College in 2009. I am currently working on my dissertation under the advisement of Stephen Neale. I have been teaching introductory philosophy at Brooklyn College for four years.
